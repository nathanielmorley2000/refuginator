What are Refugia?

When a population or species is threatened by changing environmental conditions, it faces three possible outcomes. It can:

  1. Migrate/retreat to areas with more favourable conditions
  2. Adapt to the changing conditions
  3. Succumb to the changing conditions and go extinct

Refugia occur when a population retreats into a small area relative to its initial geographic range. In contrast, a range shift occurs when a population moves into an area that is comparable to its initial geographic range. Refugia can be intrinsic, where the refugium exists within the population's initial geographic range, or they can be extrinsic, where the refugium exists externally to the population's initial geographic range.
